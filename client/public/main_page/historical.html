December 9, 1946

First meeting of the Constituent Assembly of India.

August 29, 1947

Drafting Committee was appointed, with Dr. B.R. Ambedkar as chairman.

February 21, 1948

The Draft Constitution was prepared and submitted to the Constituent Assembly.

November 4, 1948

The Draft Constitution was introduced in the Constituent Assembly.

November 26, 1949

The Constitution of India was adopted.

January 24, 1950

Final session of the Constituent Assembly was held, and the Constitution was signed.

January 26, 1950

The Constitution of India came into force, marking the beginning of the Republic of India.

May 16, 1951

The First Amendment to the Constitution of India was passed.

October 17, 1951

First general elections under the new Constitution began.

January 26, 1955

The Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act was passed, which reorganized the states on a linguistic basis.

December 18, 1976

The Constitution (Forty-second Amendment) Act, known as the "Mini-Constitution," was passed, bringing substantial changes.

August 15, 1984

The Constitution (Fifty-second Amendment) Act was passed, which introduced the anti-defection law.

January 7, 2004

The Constitution (Ninety-first Amendment) Act was passed, placing restrictions on the size of the Council of Ministers.

August 5, 2019

The Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Order, 2019, was issued, abrogating Article 370 and Article 35A.
